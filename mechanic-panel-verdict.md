# Panel Verdict — Top Game Concepts

*Sequential review: Zach Gage → Mark Brown → Nicole Lazzaro. Each saw prior work.*

---

## Consensus Top 5 (all three agree)

**97 — Pickle Hunt** (Fog of War + Scarcity Management)
Ornament tokens reveal fog OR score points — same resource, one decision, every tap.
Win moment: you spend your last token on a branch. The pickle is there. The scarcity makes it feel like a gamble that paid off.
*Lazzaro: "Essential. Everything else should be compared against this."*

**195 — Pickle Garland** (Hidden Information + Chain Scoring)
Keep stringing ornaments into a chain or bank now — the next ornament might break everything.
Win moment: two flavors — brave (extended and hit jackpot) or smart (banked at exactly the right moment). Both feel earned. Even the loss is fun.
*Lazzaro: "The cleanest emotional signature of any mechanic on the list."*

**117 — Vault Trigger** (Deduction + Pressure Plate)
Step on the plate before you've fully deduced what it activates — the moment you commit is the drama.
Win moment: you stepped on the plate, the timer started, the safe opened. You were right. Held-breath mechanic.
*Lazzaro: "Keep if the deduction layer is thin enough that commitment is the drama, not the logic."*

**171 — Chain Reaction Press** (Constraint Propagation + Pressing Luck)
Each correct guess adds a new rule; game tightens as you succeed. Blackjack/Jenga emotional DNA.
Win moment: you pressed one more time and it cascaded open a bonus reveal — the pickle visible in the cascade.
*Gage flagged: constraint display is everything — if rules pile up off-screen, the session collapses.*

**139 — Deduction Grid + Choice Branching** (Deduction Grid + Choice Branching)
Branch speculatively to generate new clues (paying a turn) vs. deduce further from what you have.
Win moment: your branch paid off — the pickle was on the line nobody else would have taken. Feels like being right at a dinner table argument.
*Brown's strongest missed pick. Lazzaro: Serious Fun — it means something that *you* figured it out.*

---

## Conditional (worth considering, execution-dependent)

**131 — Dimensional Heist** (Layered Planes + Key and Lock)
Key on spirit plane, pickle locked on physical plane — navigate both to win.
Win moment: "I had to go to another dimension to find it." Unique and shareable. Viable only if each plane is a single clean tap-layer.
*Lazzaro's missed pick. Both Gage and Brown overlooked it.*

**100 — Signal Switch** (Deduction Grid + Lever Toggle)
Toggle reveals information but may invalidate completed deductions.
Win moment: you sacrificed two confirmed cells to open a better path — felt like a deliberate trade.
*Gage: keep. Brown: borderline. Lazzaro: conditional, gamer-leaning. Skews toward experienced players.*

## Cut (consensus or strong single voice)

| Concept | Cut by | Reason |
|---------|--------|--------|
| 188 Evidence Burn | Lazzaro | Cold mechanic, diffuse win — doesn't feel like finding the pickle |
| 109 Deck Cycling + Hidden Info | Lazzaro | Memory load + wait mechanic, win moment is muddy |
| 96 Constraint Satisfaction + Rewind | Lazzaro | Relief ≠ triumph; rewind wins feel like survival not discovery |
| 153 Confidence Market | Lazzaro | Transactional, no discovery moment |
| 59 Pickle Sleuth | Brown + Lazzaro | UI fights phone form factor; zoom costs turn twice |
| 196 False Memory | Brown + Lazzaro | Multiplayer mechanic without an opponent |

---

## Lazzaro's Core Insight

*"Neither Gage nor Brown asked the most important question: what does the reveal animation feel like? The mechanic's job is to make the reveal feel deserved, not just to trigger it."*

The three mechanics where the earned-reveal and the emotional state align:
- **Pickle Hunt** — you gambled your tokens and won
- **Pickle Garland** — you chose to keep going and it paid off
- **Vault Trigger** — you committed and were right

*"Build those three, test them, cut the rest."*
