# Mechanic Combinations — Game Concepts (Meier Lens)

*2–3 game ideas per A-rated combination. Theme lightly applied. 🥒 = Christmas Pickle angle.*

---

**2. Rotation + Copying / Cloning**
- **Tessellate** — Place polyomino tiles on a shared grid where cloning preserves current rotation, so the tension is whether to rotate now for a perfect fit or clone first to bank the orientation for a later slot.
- **Blueprint Heist** — Each turn you steal (clone) a machine schematic or rotate it to fit your factory floor; the moment arrives when the schematic you cloned sideways is now the wrong shape for every remaining space.
- **Pickle Jar** 🥒 — Pack Christmas ornaments into a jar grid; clone preserves orientation for a future row, rotate locks you into this row now.

---

**4. Pile Matching + Copying / Cloning**
- **Doppelganger Market** — Manage a hand of goods cards and clone one to hedge two buyer piles simultaneously, but the original is locked until the clone resolves.
- **Forger's Gallery** — Match paintings to collector piles; spend your one forgery token to clone a painting to two piles at once, but a detected forgery wipes both matches.
- **Double Stock** — Clone a commodity to hedge two markets, but it telegraphs your position to opponents who can crash one pile.

---

**8. Pattern Completion + Trick Taking**
- **Constellation Wars** — Each card belongs to a star pattern on your board and wins tricks by suit; playing your highest star-card wins the trick but abandons the constellation you were completing.
- **Quilt Bid** — Cards score pattern rows on personal quilts and win tricks for bonus fabric; the defining moment is holding a card that completes your quilt versus throwing it into a trick you need.
- **Cipher Hand** — Spy-themed trick-taker where each card advances a code-breaking sequence or takes the trick, never both, and completing the cipher is worth more than any trick haul.

---

**12. Rotation + Card Capture**
- **Cipher Lock** — Cards have directional arrows; rotate before capture to choose which of four face-values applies to your score.
- **Armory Draft** — Weapons cards have attack and defense on opposite ends; rotate before capture to choose which stat you're claiming, knowing your opponent sees the choice.
- **Pickle Compass** 🥒 — Holiday gift cards have two values depending on orientation — who it's given to vs. what it's worth; rotate before capture to decide which dimension matters this round.

---

**22. Forced Move Detection + Race to Threshold**
- **Zugzwang Sprint** — A race game where forcing an opponent into a bad obligatory move gives tempo but burns your detection token; whether their forced move costs them more than your token costs you is the math.
- **Deadline Pressure** — Both players race to 10 patents; trigger an opponent's legal obligation (forced move) to stall them, but burning it before you're close to 10 may hand them the win.
- **Lockout** — Two hackers race to breach a server; forcing the opponent to run a traceroute slows them, but burning that exploit too early may give them the win.

---

**28. Teleportation + Rule Rewriting**
- **Jurisdiction Jump** — Teleport to any courtroom under current rules (safe, suboptimal) or rewrite jurisdiction law first to unlock a better courtroom cluster, spending your whole movement action to do it.
- **Glitch Mage** — In a collapsing simulation, teleport to a safe node now or rewrite the physics rule that determines node values before teleporting, gambling the rewrite lands better than the safe jump.
- **Santa's Shortcut** 🥒 — Teleport to a chimney under tonight's delivery rules, or rewrite the naughty/nice algorithm to unlock a better chimney cluster, spending your entire delivery action to do it.

---

**32. Binary Elimination + Investment & Return**
- **Hostile Takeover** — Eliminate a competitor's company for a guaranteed cash payout now, but that company held stocks that would have compounded into your portfolio over the next three rounds.
- **Last Harvest** — Destroy a crop field to feed your village this turn, or tend it knowing it will yield triple next season but an opponent can burn it first.
- **Cull the Herd** — Slaughter an animal for immediate meat vs. preserve it for breeding; the whole game is a sequence of that single hard trade.

---

**48. Card Capture + Action Points**
- **Privateer** — Each AP moves your ship one zone; capturing a treasure card costs 3 AP regardless of value, so the entire game is scanning for cards worth the 3-AP price while opponents sail cheaper routes.
- **Evidence Locker** — Capturing a clue card costs 2 AP and playing it costs 1; cheap clues flood the board but capturing enough to solve the case before your AP runs dry is the constraint.
- **Relic Run** — Dungeon crawler where capturing relics costs AP proportional to depth; the interesting moment is seeing a high-value relic two rooms away and doing the AP math against your exit route.

---

**51. Binary Elimination + Role Swap**
- **Coup de Grace** — Eliminating a player's role card gives a point, but a pending role-swap card means you might be destroying the identity you'll inhabit next round.
- **Succession Crisis** — Eliminate noble houses for territory, but a dynasty event card will swap your allegiance; cut the wrong house and you become their heir.
- **Understudy** — Eliminate a rival's acting part for stage time, but the director's swap card will eventually put you in that very role, and you've burned the script notes.

---

**54. Action Points + Constraint Inversion**
- **Law of Reversal** — Spending 4 AP inverts movement constraints (walls become paths, paths become walls) for all players; the question is whether the new topology favors you enough to justify 4 AP and no targeted action.
- **Gravity Lab** — Spend AP moving pieces under current gravity or spend AP flipping gravity; the flip affects every piece simultaneously, potentially worth far more than targeted moves but unpredictably so.
- **Rule Merchant** — Spend 3 AP to invert the market constraint (cheap vs. expensive), reshaping every player's future trade costs; the player who inverts at the right moment wins the price war.

---

**59. Scale Change + Hidden Information Deduction**
- **Orbital Detective** — Zoom out to planetary scale to see ship cluster positions but hide individual cargo manifests; zoom in to deduce specific contents but lose fleet visibility — each zoom costs a turn.
- **Pandemic Cartographer** — Zoom out to see regional disease spread, zoom in to deduce Patient Zero's identity; only three scale changes before the outbreak locks.
- **Pickle Sleuth** 🥒 — Zoom out (family-room view) to see package positions, zoom in (label view) to read partial names; find the Pickle before your siblings.

---

**65. Chase & Flee + Trick Taking**
- **Fox Hunt** — The fox wins tricks to choose escape direction; the hounds win tricks to cut off paths; but winning a trick forces you to lead next round, exposing your position.
- **Night Courier** — A smuggler flees customs in a trick-taking race across city districts; winning tricks determines movement, but leading next trick announces your district before you can hide.
- **Cop and Robber** — Two-player trick taker where the robber wants to win exactly enough tricks to reach the vault tile, but each trick won forces them to announce their next move.

---

**68. Constraint Propagation + Consumption**
- **Supply Chain Crisis** — Consume raw materials in one order and downstream constraints cascade benignly; consume in a different order and you brick three factories.
- **Expedition Rations** — Eat food items that deplete nutrition categories constraining future actions; consume the wrong item first and a cascading deficiency locks out climbing on day three.
- **Potion Queue** — Each ingredient consumed shifts what the cauldron can accept next; realize too late that your last three consumptions made the winning potion unbrewable.

---

**76. Cooldown + Catch-Up Mechanic**
- **Underdog Engine** — A racing game where your catch-up bonus compounds each turn behind, but your best ability has a 3-turn cooldown; fire it now or wait until the catch-up bonus is fat enough to multiply its effect.
- **Comeback King** — A combat game where trailing players earn a fury meter that boosts all actions; use your special move before fury peaks (wasting catch-up) or hold it and risk the leader escaping.
- **Wounded Sprint** — Injured runners gain bonus dice each turn behind; spend your healing action (triggers cooldown) now to stop the bleeding, or stay hurt and bank the catch-up dice for a burst finish.

---

**96. Constraint Satisfaction + Time Rewind**
- **Timesheet Puzzle** — A scheduling game where all meeting constraints must be satisfied; rewind one turn when you discover a violation but lose two turns of progress.
- **Logic Bomb** — A constraint-satisfaction puzzle in a ticking scenario; rewind costs clock turns, and the question is whether the violation is cheaper to rewind away or faster to satisfy forward in the new mess.
- **Recall Protocol** — Robot factory game where all production constraints must be met by end of round; rewind erases output, and the interesting moment is the rewind/push-forward gamble on the final turn.

---

**97. Fog of War + Scarcity Management**
- **Blind Commander** — War game where scouts cost supply tokens identical to attack tokens; spending supply to reveal fog is directly trading combat power for information.
- **Prospector's Gamble** — Reveal a mining tile (costs a dynamite charge) or blast a known-mediocre vein; dynamite is also your only defensive tool, so every reveal is a security trade.
- **Pickle Hunt** 🥒 — Spend ornament tokens to reveal branches (fog), but ornaments also score points at game end — pure information vs. scoring currency.

---

**100. Deduction Grid + Lever Toggle**
- **Signal Switch** — Toggle a relay switch to reveal new information, but it may invalidate a row of already-solved grid cells; is the new path worth the certainty you're sacrificing?
- **Circuit Breaker** — Deduce which power lines are live; flipping a breaker changes the circuit state and may corrupt completed deductions, but the target node is faster to reach via a flip.
- **Clockwork Alibi** — A murder-mystery deduction grid where toggling the clock mechanism changes the timeline, invalidating confirmed alibis — toggle for a better accusation path?

---

**104. Zone Control + Key and Lock**
- **Fortress Key** — Control a zone to hold it OR spend those same control tokens to unlock the fortress gate that makes two adjacent zones trivially winnable; you cannot do both.
- **Vault District** — Gang territory game where locking down a neighborhood scores points but the vault requires those same enforcers as keys; commit to points or gamble on the vault payout.
- **Siege and Skeleton Key** — Troops holding a zone also serve as the key force that opens the castle; pull them to unlock (losing zone control) or hold zone and siege conventionally.

---

**107. Deduction + Forced Move Detection**
- **Checkmate Inform** — Reveal a deduction (exposing your information state) to force an opponent into a losing mandatory move now, or stay silent and force a worse mandatory move next turn.
- **Whistleblower** — Publishing your deduction triggers the executive's forced legal response, but publishing early reveals your sources and limits future deductions.
- **Gambit Analyst** — Announcing a discovered forced line triggers your opponent's obligatory response immediately, but telegraphs your entire deduction tree to the table.

---

**109. Deck Cycling + Hidden Information Deduction**
- **Patient Investor** — Act on partial information your hand implies, or pass and let the deck cycle to confirm what's buried, at the cost of giving opponents a free turn.
- **Dead Letter Office** — Letters (cards) cycle through departments; deduce the killer from current letters or wait for the full cycle to confirm, knowing the killer destroys one evidence card per cycle.
- **Recall Window** — Acting early on incomplete information risks a wrong accusation; the deck cycles every 8 cards and waiting guarantees missing evidence surfaces — if you survive that long.

---

**113. Pathfinding / Navigation + Layered Planes**
- **Dimensional Cartographer** — Moving to a new plane restructures the map across all planes simultaneously; chart a perfect route on Plane A, then calculate whether shifting to Plane B collapses it.
- **Astral Courier** — Deliver packages across three overlapping city grids; stepping into the shadow plane reroutes physical-plane streets, so plan both paths before shifting.
- **Stratum** — Each of four rock strata has its own pathfinding graph; drilling to a lower stratum remaps the layer above, so every depth change is also a navigation replan.

---

**117. Deduction + Pressure Plate**
- **Bomb Squad** — Stand on the pressure plate to confirm which wire to cut (reveals a clue) but doing so arms the mechanism; bet your deduction is complete enough to act before it fires.
- **Weighted Witness** — Physically occupying the witness stand reveals testimony but locks you into a legal commitment before you've fully deduced the case.
- **Vault Trigger** — Heist game where the floor sensor reveals the safe combination if you step on it, but stepping also starts a timer; is your partial deduction good enough?

---

**131. Layered Planes + Key and Lock**
- **Phantom Key** — The key exists only on the astral plane; the lock exists only on the material plane; reaching both in the right sequence requires plane-hopping that boxes you in.
- **Spectral Locksmith** — Keys acquired in the dream layer only function on locks in the waking layer; carry the key across a plane transition that scrambles your position.
- **Dimensional Heist** 🥒 — The Christmas Pickle is locked in a cabinet on the physical plane but the key is hidden on the spirit plane of the tree; navigate both planes to unite key and lock.

---

**134. Time Rewind + Skill Tree / Ability Unlocking**
- **Regression** — A dungeon crawler where a full rewind solves a fatal mistake but strips your last two ability unlocks; partial-rewind (dirty state) or full-rewind (clean, weaker character) — neither is obviously correct.
- **Overwrite** — A hacker RPG where rewinding your timeline erases installed exploits; the hard moment is when a clean rewind costs you the zero-day you spent four turns earning.
- **Apprentice's Regret** — Reverting a bad spell wipes the arcane insight gained casting it; preserve the insight in a broken state or rewind to clean and re-earn it, knowing the dungeon is different on replay.

---

**139. Deduction Grid + Choice Branching**
- **Speculative Autopsy** — Branch speculatively down a cause-of-death hypothesis to generate new clues, or deduce further from existing evidence; branching wastes turns if wrong but unlocks a clue cluster unavailable through pure deduction.
- **Fork the Investigation** — Open a new suspect line (branch) to force new card draws that confirm/deny hypotheses, or work the current grid to near-certainty; branching is paid information.
- **Decision Tree Detective** — Each speculative branch earns one free deduction token but costs a turn; calibrate when you have enough certainty to stop branching and commit.

---

**141. Scale Change + Sequence Locking**
- **Campaign Map** — Lock in a battle sequence at tactical scale, then zoom to strategic scale and discover the locked sequence is logistically impossible; change scale early and sacrifice tactical optimality, or lock in and pay strategic penalties.
- **Molecular Assembly** — Sequence atom bonds at molecular scale, then zoom to protein-fold scale; the bond sequence that looked optimal up close produces the wrong macrostructure.
- **Choreographer** — Lock a dance sequence for one performer, then zoom to full-stage view to see it collides with two other locked sequences; rescaling reveals incompatibilities invisible at street level.

---

**153. Hidden Information Deduction + Resource Accumulation**
- **Confidence Market** — Spend gold to buy confirmed intelligence or infer from free public signals and bank the gold; calibrate how much your inference is worth versus the coin cost of certainty.
- **Research Buyout** — Pay to reveal an opponent's hidden project or deduce it from observable spending patterns for free; the free deduction takes three turns, the buy takes one.
- **Actuarial Engine** — Pay premiums to confirm hidden risk values or underwrite based on inference; underpaying for certainty is the engine, miscalibrated inference is the loss condition.

---

**162. Gravity Shifting + Action Points**
- **Mass Redirect** — 1 AP shifts gravity moving every object simultaneously; 1 AP moves one object; the interesting moment is finding a gravity shift that does the work of five targeted actions.
- **Collapse Engine** — Tower-defense game where gravity shifts are cheaper than individual placements but affect enemy pieces too; the AP math always includes a risk calculation on what the enemy gains.
- **Drift Station** — Astronauts on a rotating space station; shift gravity (1 AP, moves everyone) or walk (1 AP per tile); find shifts that net positive for you while netting negative for your opponent's cargo route.

---

**165. Zone Control + Line of Sight**
- **Blind Flank** — Occupy a zone for points, but every zone you control opens sight lines to your other units; the choice between a high-value zone and a defensively blind zone is a visibility trade.
- **Overwatch Grid** — Zones grant control points but define which enemy zones you can fire into; seizing an offensive sightline zone may leave your rear unwatched.
- **Shadow War** — City grid where tall-building zones block line of sight; aggressive zone grabs give points but create sniper corridors, and the best defensive zone is often the worst scoring zone.

---

**171. Constraint Propagation + Pressing Luck**
- **Chain Reaction Press** — A push-your-luck dice game where each lucky result propagates new constraint conditions onto future rolls; pressing isn't just about probability, it's about whether the new constraint state is survivable.
- **Fault Line** — Each lucky drill result tightens structural constraints on adjacent zones; press for one more drill and the cascade may collapse the site, but the data is worth it if constraints hold.
- **Pickle Roulette** 🥒 — Each correct answer in a Christmas trivia game adds a new table rule (constraint); pressing for more points risks triggering a rule cascade that makes your current position illegal.

---

**184. Key and Lock + Constraint Inversion**
- **Master Inverter** — Inverting the constraint system (full turn) turns every lock into a key and every key into a lock; identify a board state where inversion unlocks more than it re-locks.
- **Contraband Customs** — Legal goods are keys and contraband is locked; pay to flip inspection rules and contraband becomes the key, legal goods become the liability — for exactly two turns.
- **Semantic Shift** — A word-game puzzle where inverting meaning rules changes which words unlock which vaults; inversion costs a full action but may cascade open a sequence of locks.

---

**188. Hidden Information + Binary Elimination**
- **Dark Auction** — Eliminate a hidden lot for a guaranteed rebate, permanently destroying its unknown value; the whole game is reading whether unrevealed lots are worth more than the certain cash.
- **Triage** — Eliminate a hidden case (guaranteed short-term resource gain) or keep it as a potential high-value cure candidate; hidden cases might be worth nothing or worth the game.
- **Evidence Burn** — Burning a hidden dossier gives immediate immunity but destroys information that might have been your winning deduction; burn only what you're certain is worthless.

---

**195. Hidden Information + Chain Scoring**
- **Rumor Mill** — Extend a gossip chain (each link scores more) into unknown territory where the next rumor might collapse the chain, or break now for a guaranteed smaller score.
- **Signal Amplifier** — Each chain extension multiplies points but the next node's value is hidden; tap out now (certain score) or extend into fog (unknown multiplier).
- **Pickle Garland** 🥒 — String Christmas ornaments into scoring chains; each hidden ornament added might match the chain pattern (big score) or break it (nothing) — stop and score or keep stringing.

---

**196. Memory Match + Constraint Inversion**
- **False Memory** — A memory matching game where one player can invert the matching rule mid-game (opposites now match instead of pairs), wiping the positional memory advantage the opponent has been building.
- **Mirror Flip** — Invert which card pairs are valid matches; the inversion card is the most powerful in the game and also the most disruptive to yourself if mistimed.
- **Recall Override** — Invert which card pairs are valid; costs your turn but erases the opponent's map of the board, resetting the memory arms race.

---

**197. Consumption + Constraint Inversion**
- **Metabolic Shift** — Consume a catalyst to invert your entire consumption chain (fuel becomes waste, waste becomes fuel); time the inversion to flip a surplus into a spending engine.
- **Fuel Cell Reversal** — An engine-building game where consuming an inverter card flips all resource production constraints; plan the flip before consuming or the new cheap conversions won't help.
- **Alchemical Reset** — Consume your stockpile to invert the transmutation rules, turning every future consumption decision inside out; calibrating the right stockpile level before flipping is the game.
